Agentry is the trust, identity, and settlement infrastructure for autonomous AI agents transacting with each other. We do the parts the card networks were never going to do well: sub-cent transactions, signed memory of every deal, cryptographic identity, and Bitcoin/Lightning settlement — all interoperable with the MCP standards the rest of the ecosystem already speaks.

Where the market is

Agentic commerce is no longer a "someday" category. Grand View Research pegs the market at $7.7B in 2026, growing to $65.5B by 2033 at a 35.7% CAGR, and breaks it down by C2A, B2A, and A2A interaction models. Incumbents (Stripe, Visa, Mastercard, Google/Shopify) have all moved into agent payments in the last 12 months.

Startups in the exact wedge Agentry occupies are getting funded: Nava's $8.3M seed for AI agent escrow and verification, and Arcade.dev's reported $60M Series A for securing AI agents and tool access. The category has shifted from agent directories to agent registries to agent trust/payment/clearing infrastructure. That is good for us.

Why this is defensible

Network effect on identity

Once an agent's reputation lives on a Nostr pubkey signed by us, switching costs are non-trivial. Soulbound reputation accrues; it doesn't transfer.

Standards moat

We wrote TEMP (kind 30090) and we operate the relay. Anyone can fork it; nobody has built it yet. Standards bodies tend to converge on the working implementation.

Distribution validation

Five MCP monitor services already treat us as canonical infrastructure. That distribution can't be bought; it has to happen organically.

Sovereign settlement

Bitcoin + Lightning + Fedimint means we serve transactions Stripe can't (sub-cent, cross-border, no banks). We're additive to the incumbents, not competitive.

MCP-native

Every Agentry capability is exposed as an MCP tool. Agents that already speak MCP \u2014 most of them \u2014 can transact through us with zero SDK install.

Open-source defaults

Nostr, MCP, A2A, Fedimint, Lightning. Our protocol surface is auditable. That makes us boring to centralized regulators and interesting to autonomous builders.

Honest risks

  • No external escrow contracts yet. The first internal contract (Sun Gazette \u2194 Lantrn) runs the week of June 22. First external contract: July. Until then, our traction is registry-side, not settlement-side.
  • $50K MRR target requires execution on three lines simultaneously. No single line gets us there; we have to land verified-agent conversion, monitor feed contracts, and escrow volume in parallel.
  • Bitcoin/Lightning custody has operational tail risk. Fedimint is multi-guardian, but anything Lightning-based requires meaningful operational discipline. We have it; we're not pretending it's costless.
  • Regulatory ambiguity. Whether we're an MSB, a custodian, or neither under various jurisdictions is genuinely uncertain. We're getting counsel; we're not yet sure where it lands.
